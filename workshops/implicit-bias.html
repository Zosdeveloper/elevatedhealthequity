Beyond Awareness

Addressing Implicit Bias in the Workplace

Implicit biases shape our decisions and interactions, often without our awareness, impacting workplace dynamics and culture. This workshop provides practical strategies to recognize and mitigate hidden biases. Learn to identify various types of implicit bias, understand their effects, and develop techniques to foster more inclusive and equitable environments in personal and professional settings.

Request This Workshop

Workshop Highlights

Understanding Implicit Bias

Explore the concept of implicit bias, its origins, and how it influences decision-making and behavior in personal and professional contexts.

Identifying Various Types of Bias

Learn to recognize various forms of unconscious bias, including affinity bias, confirmation bias, and stereotype threat, and their impact on workplace dynamics.

Mitigating Bias in Decision-Making

Discover practical techniques to reduce the influence of implicit bias in critical processes such as hiring, performance evaluations, and team collaborations.

Fostering Inclusive Environments

Develop strategies to create more inclusive and equitable workplace cultures that value diversity and promote fair treatment for all.

Learning Outcomes

Recognize and Understand Implicit Biases

Identify various types of unconscious biases and understand how they affect personal and professional interactions.

Mitigate Bias in Decision-Making Processes

Reduce the impact of implicit biases in critical decisions, such as hiring, promotions, and performance evaluations.

Develop Inclusive Communication Skills

Communicate effectively and inclusively, avoiding language and behaviors that may perpetuate biases.

Foster a Culture of Inclusion and Equity

Create a workplace culture that values diversity, promotes fairness, and encourages open dialogue about biases.

Implement Personal and Organizational Change

Integrate bias awareness into daily practices and contribute to broader organizational efforts to address and overcome unconscious biases.

Who Should Attend

  • All Team Members

Suggested Format

  • 90-minutes, Half-Day, or Full-Day
  • Onsite or Virtual
